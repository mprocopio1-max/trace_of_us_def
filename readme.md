# TRACES OF US

### Every connection leaves a trace.

Interactive Installation  
**Martina Procopio — 2026**

---

## Overview

*Traces of Us* is an interactive installation inspired by mycelial networks and the concept of a sixth sense: the ability to perceive connections that exist beyond direct visibility.

The project imagines a digital system capable of recording, transforming and preserving traces of human presence over time.

Visitors interact with a plasma sphere while a camera positioned above it observes the movement of their hands. Every gesture becomes a trace. Every trace becomes memory. Every memory becomes part of a living network that continuously evolves.

The installation does not preserve identities.

**It preserves presence.**

---

## Concept

In nature, mycelial networks allow trees to exchange information through invisible underground connections.

These networks transmit resources, warning signals and environmental memory without direct contact.

*Traces of Us* explores the possibility of creating a digital equivalent:

> What if a digital network could preserve encounters the way mycelium preserves signals beneath the surface?

The installation transforms movement into memory and memory into connection.

Each visitor leaves an imprint within the system.

Future visitors encounter not the people who came before them, but the traces they left behind.

---

## Interaction

A plasma sphere acts as the physical interface of the installation.

A smartphone camera mounted above the sphere continuously tracks the movement of visitors' hands.

The system analyses:

- movement
- stillness
- duration
- repetition
- spatial trajectories

These behavioural patterns are translated into traces inside a three-dimensional generative environment.

When movement stops, the trace is archived and becomes part of the system's memory.

Over time, archived traces generate nodes and relationships, creating an evolving network of invisible encounters.

---

## What is Stored

The installation does **not** store:

- photographs
- video recordings
- faces
- identities
- personal information

The installation stores only:

- movement trajectories
- duration of presence
- pauses
- repetitions
- behavioural patterns

# Technical Implementation

## Hand Tracking

The system uses:

- TensorFlow.js
- MediaPipe Hands

to perform real-time hand tracking through a smartphone camera.

Only hand landmarks are processed.

No images are recorded or stored.

---

## Generative Environment

The visual environment is built using:

- Three.js
- WebGL
- GLSL shaders

The network exists as a dynamic three-dimensional structure composed of:

- particles
- traces
- nodes
- filaments
- procedural connections

Each archived trace contributes to the growth and transformation of the system.

The visual language takes inspiration from:

- mycelial networks
- plasma discharges
- neural structures
- astronomical maps

---

## Trace Generation

Hand movement influences the development of the network:

| Gesture Behaviour | Network Behaviour |
|-------------------|------------------|
| Horizontal movement | X-axis growth |
| Vertical movement | Y-axis growth |
| Duration / stillness | Z-axis growth |
| Repetition | Node generation |
| Similarity with previous traces | Connection generation |

When movement ceases for a defined period, the current trace is archived and becomes part of the network memory.

A new movement generates a new trace.

---

## Visual System

The visualisation is rendered using:

- additive particle systems
- procedural line geometries
- dynamic glow effects
- custom GLSL shaders
- procedural noise

Archived traces gradually fade, while the connections generated by the system become increasingly visible.

The resulting structure resembles an evolving digital ecosystem.

---

## Audio System

The sound environment is generated in real time using **Tone.js**.

Rather than producing a musical composition, the installation creates an evolving atmospheric layer inspired by electrical fields, plasma discharges and biological communication networks.

The audio engine combines:

- low-frequency sine oscillators
- filtered electrical noise
- dynamic reverb
- procedural modulation
- event-based resonances generated by network activity

The sound behaves as an acoustic extension of the visual network.

As traces accumulate and connections emerge, the sound continuously changes in density, resonance and spatial depth.

The audio is intentionally subtle and immersive, functioning as a living background presence rather than a soundtrack.

### Audio Architecture

The system uses:

- Tone.Oscillator
- Tone.NoiseSynth
- Tone.Filter
- Tone.Reverb

Visual parameters are continuously mapped to audio parameters:

| Network State | Audio Response |
|--------------|---------------|
| Trace density | Drone intensity |
| Network activity | Noise amplitude |
| Connection growth | Reverb depth |
| System agitation | Filter frequency |
| Emergent connections | Resonant pulses |

---

## Installation Setup

The installation consists of:

- Large projection screen
- Plasma sphere (Ø 20 cm)
- Smartphone camera mounted above the sphere
- Real-time generative visualisation
- Spatial audio system

The plasma sphere functions as a visible representation of an invisible field.

The camera does not observe the visitor's identity.

It observes movement.

---

## Experience

1. A visitor approaches the installation.
2. The plasma sphere makes an invisible field visible.
3. Hand movement generates a trace.
4. The trace is archived when movement stops.
5. The system compares the trace with previous ones.
6. New connections emerge.
7. The network evolves.

> Every visitor does not meet the others directly.
>
> They meet what others have left behind.

# 🇮🇹 Italiano

## Descrizione

*Traces of Us* è un'installazione interattiva ispirata alle reti miceliali e al concetto di sesto senso: la capacità di percepire connessioni che esistono oltre ciò che è immediatamente visibile.

Il progetto immagina un sistema digitale capace di registrare, trasformare e conservare nel tempo le tracce della presenza umana.

I visitatori interagiscono con una sfera al plasma mentre una videocamera posizionata sopra di essa osserva il movimento delle mani. Ogni gesto diventa una traccia. Ogni traccia diventa memoria. Ogni memoria entra a far parte di una rete in continua evoluzione.

L'installazione non conserva identità.

**Conserva presenze.**

---

## Concept

In natura, le reti miceliali permettono agli alberi di comunicare attraverso connessioni sotterranee invisibili.

Attraverso queste reti vengono trasmessi segnali, risorse e memoria ambientale.

*Traces of Us* immagina un equivalente digitale di questo fenomeno:

> E se una rete digitale potesse conservare la memoria degli incontri come il micelio conserva i segnali sotto la superficie?

L'installazione trasforma il movimento in memoria e la memoria in connessione.

Ogni visitatore lascia un'impronta che entra a far parte di un archivio collettivo.

I visitatori successivi non incontrano direttamente le persone che li hanno preceduti, ma le tracce che esse hanno lasciato.

---

## Esperienza

1. Una persona entra nello spazio.
2. La sfera al plasma rende visibile un campo invisibile.
3. Il movimento genera una traccia.
4. La traccia viene archiviata.
5. Il sistema genera nuove connessioni.
6. La rete continua a evolversi.

> Ogni visitatore non incontra direttamente gli altri.
>
> Incontra ciò che hanno lasciato.
